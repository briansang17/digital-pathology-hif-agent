Feature Measurement Methods & Rationale:

#1 Stromal TIL Score (both) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Measure: Percent of stromal area occupied by mononuclear inflammatory cells 
(lymphocytes + plasma cells) per TIIC Working Group guidelines
  Rationale: High stromal TIL score indicates a robust immune presence within 
the tumor microenvironment, suggesting an active anti-tumor immune response that
can be further enhanced by PD-1 blockade. This feature is an independent 
prognostic marker and predicts response to checkpoint inhibitors. To measure, 
quantify the density of lymphocytes within the tumor stroma, excluding tumor 
nests, expressed as a percentage of the stromal area or as cells/mm².

#2 Immunoscore (prognostic) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Measure: Quantify CD3+ and CD8+ cell densities (or lymphocytes as proxy on 
H&E) at two tumor regions: tumor core and invasive margin. Classify as 
Immunoscore 0–4 based on density percentiles.
  Rationale: A high Immunoscore reflects a strong anti-tumor immune response, 
characterized by the density and spatial distribution of lymphocytes within the 
tumor core and invasive margin. This comprehensive immune assessment is 
associated with improved overall survival and predicts response to 
immunotherapy. While typically quantified by IHC, an H&E-based estimation 
involves segmenting the tumor core and invasive margin, detecting all 
identifiable lymphocytes, and assigning a composite score based on their 
densities in these two compartments.

#3 Immune Phenotype Classification (predictive) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Measure: Classify the TME into: Inflamed (TILs throughout tumor parenchyma), 
Excluded (TILs at margin/stroma only), or Desert (absent TILs). Based on 
intratumoral vs stromal TIL distribution.
  Rationale: The immune phenotype (inflamed, excluded, desert) describes the 
spatial distribution of immune cells relative to tumor cells, directly 
influencing the efficacy of PD-1 blockade. An inflamed phenotype, characterized 
by intratumoral T-cell infiltration, predicts response to PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint 
inhibitors. To measure, classify tumors by visually assessing or using image 
analysis to determine if lymphocytes are primarily within tumor nests 
(inflamed), at the tumor periphery/stroma (excluded), or largely absent 
(desert).

#4 Intratumoral TIL Density (both) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Measure: Count of lymphocytes infiltrating tumor cell nests per unit area 
(cells/mm²) or as a percent of tumor nests with infiltrating lymphocytes
  Rationale: High intratumoral TIL density signifies active immune recognition 
and infiltration directly within tumor nests, providing a direct target 
population for PD-1 blockade to enhance anti-tumor activity. This feature 
predicts benefit from checkpoint inhibition across multiple tumor types. To 
measure, quantify the number of lymphocytes per unit area within the tumor cell 
nests, expressed as cells/mm² or as a percentage of the tumor area occupied by 
lymphocytes.

#5 Invasive Margin TIL Density (both) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Measure: Density of lymphocytes at the tumor-stroma interface (within 500 µm 
of the leading edge), reported as cells/mm²
  Rationale: A high density of lymphocytes at the invasive margin suggests an 
active immune response attempting to contain tumor spread, which can be 
potentiated by PD-1 inhibition to overcome immune evasion mechanisms at this 
critical interface. This feature is associated with improved survival and 
checkpoint inhibitor benefit. To measure, quantify the density of lymphocytes 
within a defined band (e.g., 0.5-1 mm) at the interface between the tumor and 
the surrounding normal tissue, expressed as cells/mm².
