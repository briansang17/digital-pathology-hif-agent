Feature Measurement Methods & Rationale:

#1 Tumor Cell Density (prognostic) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Measure: Count of tumor cells per unit area (cells/mm²) within the annotated 
tumor region
  Rationale: High tumor cell density is associated with aggressive tumor biology
and poor prognosis (he_tumor_cell_density). [By analogy from hypoxia] 
Belzutifan, by inhibiting HIF-2alpha, may alter tumor cell proliferation and 
survival, potentially leading to changes in tumor cell density. This feature is 
measured as the count of tumor cell nuclei per unit area within the tumor 
region, often used to normalize immune infiltrate ratios.

#2 Tumor-Stroma Ratio (TSR) (prognostic) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Annotation: Assess the hotspot area with the highest stromal proportion 
relative to tumor cells; select a representative 1–2 mm² field in the invasive 
front or core with highest stroma-to-tumor ratio.
  Measure: In QuPath or manually on H&E, estimate the proportion of stroma 
(fibrous connective tissue + non-immune cells) vs viable tumor cells in a 
representative field. TSR = stroma area / (stroma + tumor area). Classify as 
stroma-low (<50%) vs stroma-high (≥50%).
  Rationale: High tumor-stroma ratio (stroma-rich) is a pan-cancer independent 
negative prognostic factor (he_tumor_stroma_ratio). Given belzutifan's role as a
HIF-2alpha inhibitor, which can reduce VEGF, it may modulate the desmoplastic 
stroma, potentially altering the tumor-stroma ratio. This is measured as the 
proportion of tumor area occupied by tumor cells versus stromal components 
(e.g., fibroblasts, extracellular matrix, vessels) within a defined region of 
interest.

#3 Stromal Ratio (prognostic) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Measure: Fraction of the tumor region occupied by stromal tissue (stroma + 
endothelial cells) versus tumor cells, expressed as a percentage
  Rationale: High tumor-associated stroma ratio (>50%) is an independent poor 
prognostic factor across multiple solid tumors, associated with EMT and reduced 
immune infiltration (he_stromal_ratio). As a HIF-2alpha inhibitor, belzutifan's 
anti-angiogenic effects may reduce stromal components, potentially impacting 
this ratio. This is quantified as the percentage of the tumor area composed of 
stromal tissue (e.g., connective tissue, inflammatory cells, vessels) relative 
to the total tumor area.

#4 Tumor-Associated Macrophage Density (both) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Measure: Count of macrophage-like cells (large round nuclei with pale 
cytoplasm) per mm² in intratumoral and peritumoral compartments separately
  Rationale: High tumor-associated macrophage (TAM) density is associated with 
poor prognosis in most solid tumors due to M2 polarization promoting 
immunosuppression (he_macrophage_density). [By analogy from hypoxia] HIF-2alpha 
inhibition by belzutifan may reduce hypoxia-driven M2 polarization and 
recruitment of TAMs, potentially decreasing TAM density and altering the 
immunosuppressive microenvironment. This is measured by counting macrophage 
nuclei within the tumor region and normalizing by tumor area or tumor cell 
density.

#5 Tumor Necrosis Fraction (prognostic) 
  ROI: whole_section
  Annotation: Annotate all areas of coagulative necrosis within the tumor mass 
(pale, eosinophilic regions with ghost cells and no live nuclei); exclude 
treatment-related necrosis if known.
  Measure: Segment the whole tumor area on H&E. Identify necrotic regions as 
areas with loss of nuclear staining and ghost cell outlines. Report necrosis 
fraction = necrotic area / total tumor area (0–1).
  Rationale: High tumor necrosis fraction reflects intratumoral hypoxia and 
predicts poor prognosis; in anti-VEGF settings, it serves as a baseline hypoxia 
indicator and is reduced by successful vascular normalization 
(he_necrosis_fraction). Belzutifan, by inhibiting HIF-2alpha and its downstream 
pro-angiogenic effects, may reduce intratumoral hypoxia and consequently 
decrease the tumor necrosis fraction. This is measured as the percentage of the 
total tumor area exhibiting coagulative necrosis, characterized by ghost 
outlines of cells with loss of nuclear detail.
